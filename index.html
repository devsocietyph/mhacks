Mental Health Hacks was a hackathon that transformed creative media into real, concrete outputs to tell mental health stories, build resources and concepts, and more. Online and transformative, over 100 participants signed up to be a part of building a mental health story.

In the Mental Health Hacks showcase, anyone is free to view the collection of works and stories behind the event. Let these works last and touch; a reminder of the stories that we had all convened to tell.


Learn our story


How did it happen?

To ensure that all participants work within the same 72 hour time frame, which took place from June 16 to June 18, participants were garnered until a certain time, where we then closed applications. An hour prior to the beginning of the 72 hour cycle, a randomized mental health topic was sent to each participant — ranging from narcissism, teenagers, PTSD, to family issues. Works done during the duration are to relate to or be about the given topic.

Prior to and during the event, we gave the participants access to several resources given by our partners, that could help them understand and express topics concerning mental health in a well-informed and positive manner, as well as serve as reading materials for people to better understand mental health as a whole.
